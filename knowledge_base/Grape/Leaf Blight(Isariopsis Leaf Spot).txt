Crop: Grape

Class Label: Leaf_Blight

Disease: Leaf Blight

Summary:
Leaf Blight (Isariopsis Leaf Spot) is a fungal disease caused by Pseudocercospora vitis. It produces brown angular leaf lesions that enlarge and may cause premature defoliation under humid conditions.

Symptoms:
- Small angular brown leaf spots
- Dark brown lesions
- Lesions enlarge and merge
- Large blighted areas
- Leaves curl and dry
- Premature leaf drop
- Dark fungal growth beneath leaves

Immediate Action:
- Remove infected leaves
- Improve air circulation
- Reduce leaf wetness
- Prune severely infected leaves

Prevention:
- Maintain vineyard sanitation
- Remove infected debris
- Prune for better airflow
- Avoid overcrowded vines
- Improve canopy management

Treatment:
- Apply fungicides during favorable weather
- Combine fungicide application with cultural practices

Recommended Fungicides:
- Mancozeb
- Copper-based fungicides
- Chlorothalonil
- Myclobutanil
