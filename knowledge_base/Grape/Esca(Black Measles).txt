Crop: Grape

Class Label: Esca

Disease: Esca

Summary:
Esca is a serious grapevine trunk disease caused by several fungi including Phaeomoniella chlamydospora. It produces tiger-striped leaves, internal wood decay, fruit spotting, and may eventually kill the vine.

Symptoms:
- Tiger-striped leaf pattern
- Yellow or red margins around dead tissue
- Interveinal necrosis
- Black measles-like berry spots
- Shoot dieback
- Tendril dieback
- Sudden vine collapse in severe cases

Immediate Action:
- Identify infected vines early
- Remove infected trunk wood
- Protect pruning wounds
- Improve vine health with irrigation and nutrients

Prevention:
- Perform double pruning
- Protect pruning wounds
- Prune during dry weather
- Maintain balanced nutrition
- Reduce plant stress

Treatment:
- Remove infected wood below diseased areas
- Apply pruning wound protectants
- Use fungicidal wound treatments

Recommended Fungicides:
- Thiophanate-methyl
- Myclobutanil
- Tetraconazole
