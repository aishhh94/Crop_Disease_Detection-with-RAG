Crop: Grape

Class Label: Black_Rot

Disease: Black Rot

Summary:
Black Rot is a fungal disease caused by Guignardia bidwellii. It affects leaves, shoots, and berries, producing brown leaf spots with black pycnidia and causing berries to shrivel into black mummies if left untreated.

Symptoms:
- Small brown or tan spots with dark margins
- Yellow halos around leaf spots
- Black pycnidia inside mature lesions
- Brown to purple-brown berry spots
- Berries become black, shriveled mummies
- Dark elongated lesions on shoots and petioles

Immediate Action:
- Remove infected berries immediately
- Collect and destroy fallen leaves
- Prune diseased shoots
- Improve air circulation around vines

Prevention:
- Remove mummified berries
- Maintain proper vine spacing
- Prune regularly
- Ensure good sunlight exposure
- Avoid overhead irrigation

Treatment:
- Apply preventive fungicides before bloom
- Continue spraying until four weeks after bloom
- Begin treatment as early as possible

Recommended Fungicides:
- Mancozeb
- Myclobutanil
- Captan
- Copper-based fungicides
