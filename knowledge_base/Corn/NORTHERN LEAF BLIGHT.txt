Crop: Corn

Class Label: Northern_Leaf_Blight

Disease: Northern Leaf Blight

Summary:
Northern Leaf Blight is a fungal disease caused by Exserohilum turcicum. It forms large cigar-shaped gray-green to tan lesions that expand rapidly and can greatly reduce grain yield under favorable weather conditions.

Symptoms:
- Large cigar-shaped lesions
- Gray-green to tan leaf spots
- Lesions grow parallel to veins
- Lesions may merge into large blighted areas
- Dark dusty fungal spores on mature lesions
- Reduced photosynthetic area

Immediate Action:
- Scout infected fields immediately
- Check lower canopy first
- Remove heavily infected leaves if possible
- Monitor disease progression

Prevention:
- Plant resistant hybrids
- Rotate with non-host crops
- Manage crop residue properly
- Avoid continuous corn cultivation

Treatment:
- Apply foliar fungicides during early infection
- Follow recommended spray timing

Recommended Fungicides:
- Azoxystrobin
- Propiconazole
- Pyraclostrobin
- Mancozeb
