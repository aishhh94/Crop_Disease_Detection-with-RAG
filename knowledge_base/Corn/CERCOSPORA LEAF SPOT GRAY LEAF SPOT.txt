Crop: Corn

Class Label: Cercospora_Leaf_Spot

Disease: Cercospora Leaf Spot (Gray Leaf Spot)

Summary:
Gray Leaf Spot is a fungal disease caused by Cercospora zeae-maydis. It produces rectangular gray-to-brown lesions that remain restricted by leaf veins and can significantly reduce photosynthesis and grain yield if left untreated.

Symptoms:
- Small pinpoint spots with yellow halos
- Narrow rectangular gray-brown lesions
- Lesions grow parallel to leaf veins
- Block-like lesion appearance
- Large dead blighted areas during severe infection
- Brown-gray dead tissue formation
- Spore production under humid conditions

Immediate Action:
- Inspect lower leaves immediately
- Remove heavily infected leaves if possible
- Monitor disease spread weekly
- Reduce excessive field moisture

Prevention:
- Use resistant or moderately resistant hybrids
- Rotate crops away from corn
- Manage crop residue properly
- Avoid continuous corn cultivation

Treatment:
- Apply fungicides during high disease pressure
- Spray around tasseling to early silking stage

Recommended Fungicides:
- Azoxystrobin
- Pyraclostrobin
- Propiconazole
- Triazole + Strobilurin premix
