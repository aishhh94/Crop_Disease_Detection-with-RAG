Crop: Corn

Class Label: Common_Rust

Disease: Common Rust

Summary:
Common Rust is a fungal disease caused by Puccinia sorghi. It produces reddish-brown raised pustules on corn leaves and spreads rapidly under cool, humid conditions.

Symptoms:
- Small raised pustules
- Rust-red to brown lesions
- Lesions appear on both leaf surfaces
- Powdery spore release
- Yellowing around infected areas

Immediate Action:
- Remove infected leaves
- Inspect nearby plants
- Monitor disease spread regularly

Prevention:
- Use resistant varieties
- Practice regular field scouting
- Avoid excessive moisture
- Maintain proper field sanitation

Treatment:
- Apply fungicides at the early stage of infection
- Repeat spray if disease continues spreading

Recommended Fungicides:
- Azoxystrobin
- Propiconazole
